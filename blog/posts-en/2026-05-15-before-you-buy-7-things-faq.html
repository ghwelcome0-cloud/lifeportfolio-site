Before you buy — 7 things to check (FAQ)

Seven of the most common pre-purchase questions on one page. For many readers, this page helps more than the product page does.

2026-05-15 · 5 min read · FAQ

First — what kind of diagnostic is Life Portfolio?

Life Portfolio isn't a “type-classification test.” It's a custom self-management diagnostic + execution program built on a single 4-stage flow: self-understanding → self-expression → self-design → self-execution. Two PDFs are auto-issued the moment payment clears.

The Life Portfolio 4 Stages

  1. Stage 1 · Self-understanding — 76-question response patterns measure tendencies, values, strengths, weaknesses, decision patterns as data.
  2. Stage 2 · Self-expression — The data converts into a one-volume Mission Report (mission, vision, identity, core values).
  3. Stage 3 · Self-design — Recommended careers, roles, and three first actions for next week.
  4. Stage 4 · Self-execution — A 21-day routine, 3-month check-in, and 1-year vision that drop onto your calendar.

The key is that all four stages flow as one. The output isn't a “type label” — it's actions that land on next week's calendar. See Check · Interpret · Execute as One Set for the full method.

Q3. Can children under 14 use this?

Signup carries an “14+ self-declaration” checkbox. Users under 14 require a legal guardian's consent, so the self-signup flow is from age 14 onward. For EU users, GDPR Art. 8 effectively recommends age 16+.

Q4. Can I re-open my report from My Page?

Yes. Both PDFs are kept on My Page permanently, in the language you chose at issue time, and can be re-opened or re-downloaded on desktop or mobile.

Yes. Email faise@lifeportfolio.co.kr with team size and dates — we'll send a group flow and a team diagnose-and-practice guide. Q6. How is my data handled?

Responses, payment records, and issued outputs are handled per the Privacy Policy. Permanent storage on My Page is scoped to your authenticated account, and deletion is supported on request (subject to statutory retention).

Q7. How do I know the result is “accurate”?

The standard for accuracy here is not “scientific absolute truth” — it is “does it get used next week?” In other words, the test is validated by usefulness and repeatability. That is why each result ships with three first actions, a 3-week routine, 3-month check criteria, and a 1-year vision. If those four lines do not connect to your next week's calendar, the tool has failed regardless of any psychometric claim.

Design evidence

The founder has run the same exercise by hand since his university days — keeping mission, vision, identity, strengths, and core values in a Franklin-style planner, and walking through the same process with one person at a time, in person. The validation bar across that long stretch was simple: “Do I still re-open this same booklet next week?” Life Portfolio at $8.99 is that accumulated practice compressed into a single issuance.

The standard for a good tool is not scientific absoluteness, but whether you re-open it next week.

Last line — when we'd suggest not buying

  • If you're expecting a single-line “correct answer.”
  • If you're unlikely to re-open the PDF later.
  • If general advice (“try things”) is what you actually want — a free type test fits better.

If any of those three describe you, this may not be the right moment to pay. The most efficient moment to pay is the one where “I want next week to actually change” is on the table.
