So your answers become the result — notes on refining the report & program

This is not a feature announcement. It is a note about the work done where you cannot see it. Same price, same 15-minute check — but inside it, we refined the ‘uniqueness’ of the report and the program to be a little more honest. Here is what changed, and why, in plain language.

2026-06-14 · 6 min read · Notes

First, a word

We are often asked the same question: “Is this report really different for each person?” This article is our honest answer — and a record of the work we recently did to raise the quality of that answer. It is less a boast and more a report that we are keeping a promise.

Why we went back in

Life Portfolio starts from something simple: your one-line mission and vision should come from what you answered, and nothing else. Not from squeezing a person into a few preset types, but from letting your responses themselves become the raw material of the sentence.

But when you run a tool for a while, the convenient ‘molds’ you built can quietly eat away at that principle. Picking a few well-crafted phrases and slotting them in is fast and stable. The risk is that people who answered differently can end up with similar sentences. For us, that was the one thing to avoid.

The report — from ‘picking’ to ‘synthesizing’

The deepest change this time was in the report’s mission and vision sentences. Previously, part of the process picked from a pool of polished phrases closest to your tendencies and stitched them together. We removed that step. Now we synthesize a single sentence using your answers directly as the material.

  • We cut the abstract, literary language. Instead of pretty-but-vague phrasing, we use everyday verbs that show what you actually do. A mission should not be an elegant noun; it should be a verb you can act on tomorrow.
  • We use the words that came from your answers. Even ‘growth’ reads differently — for one person it is ‘the strength to analyze a problem and find a solution,’ for another ‘the strength to connect people.’ That difference now stays in the sentence.
  • We smoothed the language so it reads naturally. Down to the small grammatical seams, we tuned it so the sentence reads cleanly — not for eight million people, but for eight billion.

So if two people genuinely answered alike, getting alike sentences is correct. That is not a bug; it is honesty. But where answers differ even a little, we made sure the difference stays on the page.

The program — a ‘next step’ tuned to your rhythm

The personalized action program turns the ‘you’ from the report into daily action. This time we redesigned the ‘next step’ section.

It used to drift toward generic advice. Now, with two simple phrases — “When this cycle ends” and “For the next cycle” — it sketches your next lap around the area and vision you are focused on right now. Not a plan you use once and discard, but a spiral that grows as it turns.

A small guide, one tap away

Next to each section of the report there is a small ⓘ icon that jumps straight to the guide for that part. This time we added the same guide to the action program: the overall summary, the 3-week / 3-month / 1-year plans, the action modules, the progress board, the expected effects, the next step, and the risks. Beside each box, the answer to “how do I read this?” is right there.

A small change — but for a first-time reader, it is a guide rail. People say a good tool needs no explanation; we think it is kinder when the explanation is within reach.

The one thing we hold to

One standard runs through all of this: the way it works for one person today must keep each person’s uniqueness even when it works for eight billion.

So instead of growing a bigger book of pretty phrases, we refine the method of turning your answers into sentences. This update is not a flashy new feature on the screen. But if the one line you receive became a little more truly yours, then we did what we set out to do.

Life Portfolio is less a ‘finished product’ and more a tool trying to keep getting honest. Right where you write your answers, so those answers become a blueprint as they are — we will keep refining the parts you cannot see.
